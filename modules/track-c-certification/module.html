Certification prep

The exam for the Databricks Certified Generative AI Engineer Associate. This hub maps the 18 modules you built onto the official 6-section blueprint, weights your study time, and hands you a clickable readiness checklist. The exam tests applied judgment on the Databricks stack, not trivia.

01 · The big idea

Study to the weights, not to the module numbers

45 scenario questions in 90 minutes, no documentation. You pass by picking the best approach under a constraint — and by studying the high-weight sections hardest.

  • C.1 — Format + blueprint. 45 questions, 90 minutes, $200 USD, online-proctored, 2-year validity. Six official sections; more than half the exam is build + deploy.
  • C.2–C.9 — Section → module map. Every official section routes to the exact modules you already built. Explore it below.
  • C.10 — Practice. The companion mock-exam.md has ~32 original, exam-style questions distributed by weight, each with an answer, rationale, and mapped module.
  • The finish line is the exam + the C4 capstone. The certificate proves you know it; the Module-17 capstone proves you can build it.

Section names, weights, fee, and passing score can change between exam versions — confirm on the live exam guide at databricks.com/learn/certification/generative-ai-engineer-associate. 05 · Field notes

What to remember

📌 ImportantStudy to the weights. More than half the exam is Application Development (30%) and Assembling and Deploying (22%). Governance is only 8%. The exam is applied — practice picking the best approach under a constraint in ~2 minutes, with no documentation.
💡 Tip · fieldTake one full timed mock early to find weak sections, then spend hours where the weights and your gaps overlap. Keep a "current-vs-legacy names" flashcard — most trap distractors are old names. Finish the C4 capstone: it is revision and the portfolio artifact you show after you pass.
⚠️ GotchaPassing score is not in B2 — do not state 70% as fact (live re-check pending). 6 official sections, not 8 — the ROADMAP's 8-domain view is a reading order, not the exam structure. Answer with current names: mlflow.genai.evaluate (not mlflow.evaluate(model_type=...)), ResponsesAgent (not ChatModel), AI Search with SDK databricks-vectorsearch (no databricks-ai-search), databricks-langchain.
06 · Notes & self-check

Test yourself

Reveal 5-question self-check
  1. How many questions, how long, and how many official sections? Which two sections together make up more than half the exam?
  2. Name the six official sections and their weights. Which is largest, and which modules cover it?
  3. Is the passing score stated in the study guide? What should you do about it before you register?
  4. Give two legacy names that would be wrong answers today, and the current name for each.
  5. Which built-in evaluation scorer requires ground truth, and which blueprint section tests that?
